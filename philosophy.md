# Engineering philosophy

## Keep software understandable

Maintainability is a feature. I prefer building systems that another developer can
understand months or years later, without having to recover essential context from
tribal knowledge.

That preference shapes the tradeoffs I make:

- simplicity over cleverness
- architecture over frameworks
- readability over brevity
- maintainability over shortcuts
- documentation over tribal knowledge
- long-term thinking over quick fixes

## Reduce complexity deliberately

One of the things I enjoy most about engineering is reducing complexity through
thoughtful design. I break problems into well-defined pieces before writing code and
look for abstractions that clarify the model rather than merely hide it.

Good design does not need to be impressive. It needs to be clear, consistent, and
explicit. I prefer a simple solution that can grow with its requirements over a
complicated one that only appears sophisticated.

## Understand the machinery

I value mastering fundamentals before chasing trends. Much of my learning focuses on
why technologies were designed the way they were—whether the subject is a database,
programming language, operating system, network, or development tool.

Understanding the machinery makes tradeoffs easier to see and explanations easier to
trust.

## Document the reasoning

Documentation is part of the engineering process, not an artifact produced after it.
Architecture documents, design notes, ADRs, and implementation plans preserve the
reasoning that code alone cannot express.

Consistency supports the same goal. Clear repository structures, naming conventions,
and recurring patterns reduce the context each contributor has to reconstruct.

## Put principles before tools

Technologies matter because of the problems they help solve. They are not a substitute
for an engineering point of view. When making decisions, I value clarity, consistency,
explicitness, strong abstractions, and thoughtful tradeoffs over allegiance to a
particular framework or trend.
